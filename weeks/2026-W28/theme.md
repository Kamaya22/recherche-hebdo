# Week 2026-W28 Theme: Urban Heat Islands and Climate Adaptation of Cities

## Why it matters

Urban areas across the globe are warming faster than the planet as a whole, driven by the urban heat island (UHI) effect. As cities replace vegetation and permeable soil with concrete, asphalt, and steel, they fundamentally alter the local energy budget: less water evaporates, more solar energy is absorbed and stored, and a dense lattice of buildings traps outgoing radiation. The result is that city centres can run 2–7°C warmer than surrounding rural areas — a gap that widens fatally during the heatwaves made more frequent by climate change. Understanding UHI matters enormously: more than 55% of humanity now lives in cities, a share projected to exceed 68% by 2050, and extreme heat is already the deadliest meteorological hazard in many countries. This week we examine the full arc of the problem — from the physics of why cities overheat to the design interventions being deployed to cool them — and ask how much is actually achievable given an accelerating climate.

## Tentative 7-day outline

- **Day 1 — Overview**: What is the urban heat island effect? The three UHI types, core physical mechanisms, typical magnitudes, and why the phenomenon matters for health and policy.
- **Day 2 — Energy budget**: The physics in depth — albedo, evapotranspiration, the urban canyon effect, thermal mass, and anthropogenic heat; how changing each factor drives temperature.
- **Day 3 — Health impacts**: Heat-related mortality and morbidity — epidemiological evidence, how the UHI amplifies heatwaves, and the populations most at risk.
- **Day 4 — Environmental justice**: Why heat exposure is not distributed equally; the role of redlining, disinvestment, and urban greening inequity in shaping who bears the burden.
- **Day 5 — Design solutions**: Green roofs, cool pavements, urban forests, blue infrastructure — the mitigation portfolio, quantified benefits, and real-world deployments.
- **Day 6 — Climate change interaction**: How global warming and UHI compound each other; future projections for cities; the limits of adaptation; city-level mitigation co-benefits.
- **Day 7 — Synthesis**: What is settled vs. open; the 3–5 biggest unanswered questions; where the field is heading; pointers for going deeper.
